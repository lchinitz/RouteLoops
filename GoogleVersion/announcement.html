RouteLoops Announcement

RouteLoops.com has been creating closed-loop routes for many years now, and to all of our users over that time we say, thank you.

The primary RouteLoops.com site uses Google Maps, and the Google Maps APIs, to generate and present routes. Over time, that has become more and more expensive, and since we provide the RouteLoops service for free to our users, we are no longer able to offer the Google-Maps-based interface.

There are other versions of RouteLoops built on top of other mapping services (Open Street Maps, and MapBox), and we have chosen to continue offering the free RouteLoops.com service using one of those versions.

Other options to get RouteLoops

RouteLoops.com has been fortunate, over the years, to work with several companies interested in offering closed-loop routing for outdoor activities. Among the best of these has been Garmin, which has built RouteLoops into their Round-Trip Routing offering. Many Garmin devices support Round Trip Routing, and with an account you can build Round-Trip Routes inside of Garmin Connect.

Announcing the open-sourcing of the RouteLoops code

Although we have chosen not to continue providing the Google Maps version of RouteLoops, with the expiration of the RouteLoops patent (7,162,363) we have decided to put the RouteLoops.com code into the public domain. If someone would like to create a new site using either the concept, or the code, you are welcome to do that. If you do, please inform the wider RouteLoops community.

Example, but highly functional, RouteLoops code is now available on github. There are three versions of the code:

  1. Google Maps version
  2. Open Street Maps version using Leaflet
  3. MapBox version
